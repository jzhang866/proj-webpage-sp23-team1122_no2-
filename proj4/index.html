2.1 Describe the effects of changing the spring constant ks; how does the cloth behave from start to rest with a very low ks? A high ks?

ks refers to the spring constant determineswho stiff the spring is. The smaller ks constant is, the more stretch / flexible the cloth is and will wave more / bounces for longer. The larger ks will present the cloth to be stiff with less wrinkles.

Small ks = 5, flexible with more wrinkles

Large ks = 50000, stiff with less wrinkles

2.2 Density

Density represents how much weight / mass each point mass hold, which will be applied as forces on each point mass / spring. The larger density is, more weight / forces will be applied to the simulated cloth and there will be larger / more obvious wrinkles shown on the cloths, and the overall cloths will be dragged towards the ground more. The smaller density is, less weight / mass are calculated and less force on the spring, so less wrinkles will be displayed.

Small density = 2, less mass / weight, small wrinkles

Normal density = 15, some wrinkles are shown

Large density = 100, huge wrinkles are shown, pulled more towards the ground

2.3 Damping

Damping refers to how much energy is lost during the spring oscillations due to friction or heat loss. The larger damping is, more energy is lost in the process between different steps, the cloths will drop and become still faster and bounce less. The smaller damping is, less energy is lost between each step and the cloths will hang and bounces back and forth for longer.

Small damping = 0.02, more bounces and more wrinkles

5.1 A shader program consists of two parts, vertex shader and fragment shader. It's a program that runs in parallel on GPU and the output of the vertex shader would be the input of the fragment shader. In vertex shader, we can calculate to obtain the corresponding coordinate in the world space and the screen space given the coordinate in the model space. We can also modify their geometric properties here. In the fragment shader, we would use it to get the color and the lighting of the given fragment(pixel).

5.2 Blinn-Phong shading model consists of three parts, ambient lighting, diffuse lighting and specular lighting. The ambient lighting is a hypothetical uniformly-distributed lighting. Diffuse lighting follows diffuse shading formula while specular lighting follows specular shading formula. Lighting in the Blinn-Phong shading model is the weighted sum of these three types of lighting

Bump mapping changes the normal of the object's surface to change the light on that pixel, making the rendered image more realistic. Displacement mapping even changes the position of vertices further in the vertex shader which would change the geometry of the object to make it more realistic. We can find that after the displacement mapping, the edge of the sphere is no longer a perfect circle, instead we can see some bump in the shape, while the periphery of the sphere after bump mapping is still a perfect circle.
